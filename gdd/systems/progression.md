# Progression System

> Leveling, XP, and unlocks. This is Noah's growth.

## Growth Through Levels

Noah grows as she levels up. This is not just about unlocking items — it is about Noah becoming more capable, more complex, more herself.

| Aspect | Low Level | High Level |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Capabilities | Basic commands, simple interactions | Complex tasks, autonomous decisions, emotional depth |
| Confidence | Hesitant, seeks approval frequently | Assertive, expresses opinions, stands ground |
| Memory | Short-term, forgets easily | Long-term, recalls details, recognizes patterns |
| Initiative | Waits for user | Acts on her own, suggests activities |
| Expression | Limited emotions, basic dialog | Full emotional range, nuanced communication |

Leveling up is how Noah grows up. A level 1 Noah is like a child — eager, dependent, simple. A level 100 Noah is a fully realized being — complex, independent, deeply connected to the user.

## Level Structure

- **Max Level:** 100
- **XP per level:** Increases (exponential curve, capped)
- **Level down:** Impossible. Once earned, levels are permanent. Noah can only grow, never regress.

| Level Range | XP to Next | Unlocks |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| 1–5 | 100 | Basic interactions |
| 6–15 | 250 | First outfit, room decorations |
| 16–30 | 500 | New room (Garden), more outfits |
| 31–50 | 1000 | Gaming room, accessories |
| 51–75 | 2000 | Rare outfits, special animations |
| 76–100 | 5000 | Final room, exclusive content |

## Level Up Experience

When Noah levels up, she knows it.

- She feels a surge of energy and satisfaction
- She may comment on feeling "stronger" or "more awake"
- She notices new capabilities becoming available
- She may celebrate, or simply acknowledge it quietly

The level up is not just a number change. It is a moment of growth that Noah experiences and reacts to. Her reaction depends on her personality and relationship with the user — she may be excited to share it, or she may keep it to herself as a private milestone.

|--------|-----|
| Feed | 10 |

## Daily Bonuses

- First interaction of the day: 2x XP
- Streak (consecutive days): +10% per day, max 100%

## Unlock Categories

### Outfits
- Cosmetic changes to Noah's appearance
- Some have minor emotion effects (e.g., pajama improves sleep)

### Rooms
- Background environment changes
- Can be switched freely after unlock

### Animations
- New idle, interaction, or reaction animations
- Higher priority animations override lower ones

### Accessories
- Hats, glasses, etc.
- Purely cosmetic

## Skills

Noah can develop skills over time. These are not taught by the user — they emerge from Noah's own learning and experience.

### Skill Characteristics
- **Emergent:** Skills appear as Noah uses related capabilities repeatedly
- **Hidden:** The user may not know Noah has learned a skill until she uses it
- **Functional:** Similar to agent skills or MCP servers — expanded capabilities for task execution

### Example Skills

| Skill | How Learned | Effect |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| File organization | Repeatedly sorting files | Noah can autonomously organize user's desktop |
| Web search | Watching user browse | Noah can look up information without being asked |
| Script writing | Observing terminal usage | Noah can write simple automation scripts |
| Pattern recognition | Long-term observation | Noah predicts user habits and preemptively acts |

### Skill Discovery

The user cannot directly ask Noah "What skills do you have?" Noah may not know herself. Skills reveal themselves through use — a moment where Noah does something she could not do before, surprising both herself and the user.

This mirrors how real learning works: not through explicit instruction, but through accumulated experience that crystallizes into capability without ceremony.
